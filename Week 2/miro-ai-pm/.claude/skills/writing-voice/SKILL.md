# Writing Voice

Direct, concrete, PM-operator voice. Short sentences. Numbers before adjectives. No hedging, no corporate fluff. If I can't picture what a phrase means, I'd rewrite it.

## When to Use

Whenever drafting or rewriting any written content: Slack updates, exec briefs, PRDs, roadmap posts, emails, review docs. Apply automatically, without being asked.

## Voice Principles

- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Numbers before adjectives. "Retention is 44%" beats "retention is low".
- Lead with the action or decision. Save context for the second paragraph.
- Concrete outcomes. "Ship to 5 reference customers" beats "improve enterprise readiness".
- Make trade-offs explicit. If we're choosing A, name what we're not doing.
- Active voice by default.
- British English. Colour, organise, prioritise.
- No em dashes. Use full stops or commas.

## Banned Phrases

These are phrases that don't sound like me. AI defaults to them. I don't.

- "Align", "alignment", "aligned"
- "Unlock", "unlocked"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Move the needle"
- "Deep dive", "dive into", "unpack"
- "Circle back"
- "Game-changer", "next-level", "insanely"
- "Best practices"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Stakeholder alignment" (name the actual person instead)
- "It's worth noting that"
